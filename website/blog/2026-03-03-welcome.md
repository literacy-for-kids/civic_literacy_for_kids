Many children grow up in communities without understanding how those communities work.

They follow rules at school and at home. They hear about laws, voting, and government. But they rarely learn **how decisions are made** or **how they can participate**.

That realization is why I built this curriculum.

---

## The Problem I Kept Seeing

Kids are surrounded by civic systems every day — schools, neighborhoods, local government — but they often don't understand:

- how rules and laws are created
- how communities make decisions together
- what rights and responsibilities citizens have
- how to think critically about fairness and justice
- how they can make their voice heard

Many kids feel like government and civic life are things that happen **to them**, not things they can be **part of**.

That gap motivated this project.

---

## What I Believe Kids Should Learn Instead

Civic literacy shouldn't just be about memorizing facts about government.

Instead, kids should develop **understanding and habits of mind** that stay useful for life.

Things like:

- communities are made of **people working together**
- rules and laws exist for **reasons that can be discussed**
- citizens have **rights and responsibilities**
- decisions affect different people in **different ways**
- participation is how communities **improve**

Those ideas help children develop **confidence and curiosity** about the world around them.

---

## Why I Made It Public

I originally created this curriculum for my own teaching and mentoring.

But the more I worked on it, the more I realized:

Many parents and educators want to teach civic skills… but **don't know where to start**.

So I decided to publish the full curriculum online for anyone to use.

If it helps even a few kids discover that civic life is something they can **understand, engage with, and help shape**, then it was worth it.
